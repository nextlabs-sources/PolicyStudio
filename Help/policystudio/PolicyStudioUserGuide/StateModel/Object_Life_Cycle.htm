Policy Studio provides a process that guides you through the life cycle of a policy or component, from initial creation through deploy­ment and eventual retirement. Whenever you are working with a policy or com­ponent in the Policy Author editing pane, the current life cycle stage of the policy or component is displayed in the control bar at the bottom of the window.

At any given time, every policy or policy component is in one of seven possible statuses, depending on what action you take when working in Policy Author.

 

Control bar displaying status of the policy or component

The following table describes these statuses, listed in the sequence that repre­sents their life cycle. The Status column in the table represents both the status as displayed in the life cycle control bar in Policy Author, and in the Status col­umn in the Object grid in Policy Manager. The State column refers to the State tab the object will display on in Policy Manager; note that an object can appear on more than one tab simultaneously. All actions mentioned in the User Action column are performed in Policy Author.

Once an object is in any state past Draft, it is present for viewing and managing in the Deployment History window. There, you can track what version is cur­rently deployed, as well as all details of version history (see Comparing to the Deployed Version).
