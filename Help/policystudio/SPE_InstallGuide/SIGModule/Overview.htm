Overview

The SIG Reporting Module comprises a set of predefined bar-graph reports that you simply import into your platform and run in Reporter, plus a pair of custom obligations you will need in order to construct the policies that will gather the data to populate these reports.

Because they are based on custom policies that you can define to be triggered under highly specific conditions, these reports let you monitor with extremely fine granularity how individual users of various categories are accessing and using various classes of SharePoint data. Or, if you prefer, you can capture all the SharePoint activity in your enterprise, and then filter it by SharePoint site or department to which users belong, or the kind of business processes users are engaged in as they use SharePoint data; or you can view activity trends over time.

After you import the SIG custom obligations and SIG reports into your system, the basic sequence of using the module is as follows:

  1. Design, construct and deploy SIG policies. These are policies that capture data for SIG reporting whenever some users perform some actions rele­vant to SharePoint data. What those actions are, and whether users are blocked or allowed, and what other obligations may be triggered, are entirely up to you.

  2. Whenever a SIG policy is enforced by the SharePoint Server Enforcer, the SIG obligations log detailed data on SharePoint activity.   

  3. After enough time has passed for the deployed policies to log a usable amount of data, run the reports you are most interested in, with whatever filters best suit your needs. The resulting reports can then be used to fine-tune SharePoint permissions policies, identify dangerous or anomalous usage patterns, provide insight into how data is being used over the course of its lifecycle.
